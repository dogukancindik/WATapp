Why Work and Travel?

The Work and Travel program offers an unparalleled opportunity for university students to gain valuable life experiences while exploring the United States. By participating in this program, students not only have the chance to earn money but also immerse themselves in a new culture, gain international work experience, and improve their language skills.

One of the main benefits of Work and Travel is the opportunity to experience a new culture. By working in the U.S., students can interact with people from diverse backgrounds, visit iconic landmarks, and discover the unique aspects of American life. This exposure broadens their worldview and fosters global understanding.

In addition to cultural exchange, students gain work experience that can significantly enhance their resumes. Whether working in retail, hospitality, or food service, the skills developed during the program are transferable to many industries and are highly valued by employers worldwide.

Another major advantage of the Work and Travel program is the opportunity to improve language skills. Since participants are working in English-speaking environments, they have ample opportunities to practice and refine their English, both in professional and social settings.

The program also promotes financial independence. Participants are able to earn money to cover their travel expenses and even save for future endeavors. The money earned helps to offset the costs of the program, including travel and living expenses, making it an affordable way to explore a new country.

Lastly, Work and Travel encourages personal growth. Living and working in a foreign country teaches students how to adapt to new situations, solve problems on their own, and gain a sense of independence that will stay with them throughout their lives.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the age limit for the program?

Participants must be between the ages of 18 and 30 to be eligible for the Work and Travel program.

2. Do I need to speak English to apply?

A basic level of English proficiency is required for the program. Applicants will need to communicate with employers and coworkers in English.

3. Can I work before or after the program?

The Work and Travel program allows students to work only during the summer months. The visa allows you to enter the U.S. before your work starts and stay a bit after the program ends for travel purposes.
