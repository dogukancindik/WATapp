Accommodation & Meals

One of the key aspects of the Work and Travel program is accommodation and meals, as students will be living and working in the United States for a summer. While this experience can be exciting, understanding the options for housing and food is crucial for a smooth and enjoyable stay. Below are some details regarding accommodation and meals during your time in the U.S.

1. Employer-Provided Accommodation

Many employers participating in the Work and Travel program offer housing to their employees. This is often the most convenient option, as it ensures that students have a place to stay that is close to their workplace. Employer-provided accommodation can vary from shared apartments to dormitory-style rooms, and sometimes even cabins or other housing arrangements, depending on the employer and location. The cost of employer-provided accommodation may be deducted from your paycheck, so be sure to clarify this with your employer before arriving. Additionally, the quality and comfort of the housing can vary, so it’s important to ask about the details and photos of the accommodation before committing.

2. Finding Your Own Accommodation

If your employer does not offer housing, or if you prefer to find your own accommodation, there are several options available. You can look for rentals through online platforms such as Airbnb, Craigslist, or local rental websites. Some students also choose to stay in hostels or shared apartments with other Work and Travel participants. It’s important to consider proximity to your workplace, as well as the overall cost of rent, utilities, and transportation when choosing your accommodation. Make sure to book your accommodation well in advance, especially if you’re going to a city with a high demand for housing.

3. Meal Options

When it comes to meals, students have several options. If you’re living in employer-provided accommodation, your employer may provide meals as part of the housing arrangement. For instance, some employers who work in hospitality or food service industries offer free or discounted meals to their employees. However, in other cases, you may need to budget for your own food.

If you’re living independently, you’ll need to buy groceries and cook for yourself. The cost of food in the U.S. can vary depending on where you live, so it’s important to create a food budget. You can save money by shopping at local grocery stores, preparing simple meals at home, and packing lunch for work. If you're in a city with a large international community, you may also find affordable ethnic grocery stores that offer ingredients at lower prices.

4. Budgeting for Accommodation and Meals

When planning your budget for your time in the U.S., it’s essential to account for both accommodation and meal costs. Your accommodation expenses will depend on whether your employer provides housing or if you choose to find your own. If you're paying for housing yourself, make sure to factor in rent, utilities, and transportation costs to get to your workplace.

For meals, plan to spend an average of $200–$300 per month on food, depending on where you're living and your personal eating habits. You can save money by cooking meals at home, using public transportation instead of owning a car, and seeking out affordable food options like food trucks, discount stores, or local markets.

5. Cultural Experience: Trying Local Foods

While budgeting for meals is important, don’t forget to enjoy the local food culture! The U.S. is known for its diverse cuisine, with influences from around the world. During your time in the country, take the opportunity to try local dishes and explore food markets, food festivals, or neighborhood eateries. Many cities offer affordable street food or small restaurants that provide delicious, authentic meals at low prices.

Conclusion

Accommodation and meals are important aspects of your Work and Travel experience. Whether your employer provides housing or you find your own, it’s essential to plan ahead and budget for your stay. By considering all your options and being mindful of your expenses, you can ensure that you’ll have an enjoyable, fulfilling experience during your time in the United States. Don’t forget to embrace the cultural aspects of food and lodging, as they can greatly enhance your journey.
