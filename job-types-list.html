Job Types and Job List

The Work and Travel (WAT) program offers students an incredible opportunity to work in the United States during their summer break. Here are some typical job types and examples of jobs you may come across during your WAT experience:

Popular Job Types

  • Retail Jobs: Work as a cashier, store assistant, or in customer service. These jobs provide excellent interaction with customers and a chance to improve your communication skills.
  • Food Service: Jobs such as waitstaff, cooks, dishwashers, and baristas. You will gain hands-on experience in the hospitality industry, while earning tips and learning the ins and outs of the restaurant business.
  • Amusement Parks: Work at amusement parks as ride operators, ticket sellers, or entertainers. This is one of the most popular choices for WAT participants, offering an exciting and dynamic work environment.
  • Summer Camps: Become a camp counselor, activities coordinator, or camp instructor. These jobs are great for those who enjoy working with children and love outdoor activities.
  • Hotel & Resort Jobs: Positions like housekeeping, front desk staff, or maintenance. These roles offer great exposure to the tourism and hospitality industries.

Examples of Job Offers

1. Waitstaff at a Beach Resort

Location: California, USA

Job Description: Work as a server in a popular beach resort restaurant. You will be responsible for taking orders, serving food, and ensuring customer satisfaction. Tips are a major part of the income.

Hourly Wage: $12 per hour (plus tips)

2. Amusement Park Ride Operator

Location: Orlando, Florida, USA

Job Description: As a ride operator, you will ensure the safety of guests, help with ticketing, and manage the flow of people on the rides.

Hourly Wage: $10 per hour

3. Camp Counselor

Location: New York State, USA

Job Description: You will be leading camp activities such as swimming, hiking, and arts & crafts for children aged 7-16.

Hourly Wage: $9 per hour

Average Earnings

The average salary for WAT participants varies based on location, type of job, and tips. However, typical earnings range from $9 to $15 per hour. Additionally, many jobs offer tips, which can significantly increase your income. It is important to consider both your hourly rate and the potential for tips when evaluating job offers.

Job Benefits

  • Opportunity to explore the U.S. while working and earning money.
  • Gain work experience that can enhance your resume.
  • Meet new people and build lasting friendships from all over the world.
  • Learn valuable communication, teamwork, and customer service skills.
